Knowledge node — regulation

Canmore STR regulations (stacked reality)

Town rules + land use + strata — not a single checkbox.

Direct answer: STR revenue is only safe to model after municipal, land-use, strata, and insurance gates clear. If STR must be zero in a stress case, net cash flow usually goes Negative Carry — losing money monthly — regardless of glossy ADR.

For Bow Valley comparisons, read Canmore vs Banff. Occupancy and rate stress bands on this site often repeat ~55%–75% and ~$250–$450 nightly before discounting.

What layers govern short-term rentals in Canmore?

Municipal licensing and land-use expectations interact with condominium bylaws that can restrict or cap rentals regardless of town policy. Insurance and lender covenants add a fourth practical gate.

Why must viability models treat uncertain STR as binary risk?

If STR revenue is uncertain, underwriting should run STR-off scenarios (long-term rent or zero guest income). Partial optimism is how investors lose deposits — the knowledge graph treats regulation as a first-class node.

How does this connect to building-specific risk?

Strata votes can change rental permission faster than municipal code. Read condo risk guide alongside this page.
