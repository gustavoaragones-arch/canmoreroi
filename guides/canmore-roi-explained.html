Canmore ROI explained

Short answer: On this site, Canmore ROI means estimated monthly cash flow from STR gross minus simplified recurring costs — not appreciation. Most investors get this wrong by mixing “price went up” with “the property pays for itself.” Numbers look good on paper, but financing and HOA often eat the story.

Is Canmore a good investment for monthly cash flow?

It can be — for buyers who underwrite conservative occupancy, real fees, and sensible leverage. This is where deals break in Canmore: STR demand is real, yet purchase basis and HOA lines routinely erase thin margins.

Treat “good investment” as three separate questions: monthly solvency, downside if STR softens, and long-term wealth — not one headline from a listing site.

What does “ROI” mean on CanmoreROI.com?

Estimated monthly cash flow: guest revenue minus recurring costs that hit your account each month. We intentionally exclude appreciation and principal paydown from that headline — they do not pay this month’s shortfall.

How much income can a Canmore Airbnb generate (gross)?

Mechanically: nightly rate × occupied nights. Monthly shorthand: nightly rate × occupancy (decimal) × 30. Example: $350 at 60% occupancy → about $6,300 gross before fees and cleaning.

A 10-point occupancy swing at the same rate moves gross by about 10% — enough to flip break-even deals negative when STR net income was already thin.

How are financing and operating costs modeled?

Full ownership includes mortgage cash out, condo fees, insurance, utilities, management, reserves, and taxes. The public calculator uses a simplified bundle (financing on borrowed dollars plus a flat operating reserve) so you can stress-test fast.

This is where most investors get this wrong: they run best-case expenses, then a special assessment or insurance jump appears — this is where deals break in real buildings, not in spreadsheets.

How should I validate a deal before I offer?

Run three stresses: occupancy −10 points, nightly rate −15%, condo fees +$200/month. If only the rosy column works, you are betting on perfection.

Frequently asked questions

Is Canmore Airbnb profitable on a monthly cash basis?
Sometimes — gross bookings can look strong while net cash flow is thin or negative after financing, condo fees, management, and realistic occupancy. Profitability is a function of purchase price, fees, and nights booked — not vibes.
What does Canmore ROI mean on this site?
Here it means estimated monthly cash left after a simplified STR revenue model and a bundled monthly cost line (financing plus operating reserve), not appreciation or tax optimization.
Do Canmore condos have high fees that break ROI?
Often yes relative to Canadian norms — strata fees, insurance, and special assessments can erase paper yields. Always verify from strata documents, not listing summaries.
What are the risks of investing in Canmore STR?
Occupancy misses, fee spikes, rate changes, competitive ADR pressure, and regulatory or strata restrictions on short-term rentals. This is where deals break before appreciation helps.
Is this investment advice?
No. It is a directional model to sanity-check deals before you buy; verify every assumption with your lender, accountant, and local property manager.
