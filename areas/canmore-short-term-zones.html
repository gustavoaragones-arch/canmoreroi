Short-term rental zones (Canmore)

Short answer: STR permission in Canmore stacks municipal rules, land use, and strata bylaws — uncertainty is not a rounding error. If STR revenue must be zero in a stress case, most pro formas collapse; this is where deals break overnight when enforcement or insurance catches up.

Can you run an Airbnb legally in every Canmore condo?

No. Strata bylaws and municipal requirements can conflict with what a listing implies. Most investors get this wrong by asking only the listing agent.

After rules are clear, model cash flow using downtown condo and Solara 2BR as product anchors.

What happens to ROI if STR rules tighten?

Revenue models can collapse — always run a long-term rental fallback and insurance stress. This is where deals break in Canmore when buyers assumed “everyone does it.”

How much income is realistic if STR is clearly allowed?

Where STR is permitted and professionally operated, modeled gross for 1–2BR often tracks roughly $4,200–$9,000/month depending on quality and calendar — net still depends on fees and financing.

What are the risks of ignoring zoning and bylaws?

Fines, forced cessation, insurance denial, and lender issues. Numbers look good on paper until legal STR status disappears — verify with counsel and the corporation, not forums.

FAQ

Is this page legal advice?
No — confirm STR eligibility with qualified counsel, the municipality, and your strata corporation.
Is Canmore Airbnb profitable if rules are uncertain?
Do not model uncertain STR as certain revenue — treat ambiguity as binary risk to net cash flow.
What happens if STR rules tighten?
Revenue models can collapse; always run a long-term rental fallback scenario.
Do Canmore condos have high fees?
Often yes — and legal STR buildings can still carry heavy HOA and insurance lines.
